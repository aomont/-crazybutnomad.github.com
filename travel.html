TRAVELS










What I mean by "travel"

We all know what traveling is... Everyone does it? Well, it depends on how you define it! Let me be clear : holidays and vacations are not travel. Many people take holidays. Many people want to get away, to go on vacations. It's quite normal : people enjoy the respite, the break from the everyday routine. There's also novelty, and the excitement that comes with it.

Traveling is more than just getting away

That's not travel though. That's holidays. Inextricably linked to the enjoyment of those holidays is the existence of the routine you're getting away from.

Travel, according to me, is relinquishing that routine, that home, for a more or less extended period of time. It can be any period of time from a few weeks to many decades,










When I got the chance to travel

I've had the chance of taking holidays almost every year. However, these were short trips, by no means traveling as described above. I really got the chance to travel last year, and it was quite a journey. Between graduating from McGill and coming back to France, I was without a house and without a home for the better part of 12 months.

There were many parts to my journey. However, it was always the same kind of travel. I rarely slept twice in the same place, as I was camping, couchsurfing, or staying in hostels. I was getting around using whichever way I could, sometimes hitch-hiking, biking for a long stretch of the road, and most of the time on trains, buses, ferries and planes (quite rarely, when put in perspective). And that was an essential part of the fun.
