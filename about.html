BIO

I am French, born and raised. I spent my early years between Paris, where we lived, and Normandy, where my family has strong ties. Much of my childhood was split between the two. I'd stay up just as late waiting for my grandfather's combine harvester to come home than to watch Paris Saint-Germain play.

After finishing high school, I moved to Montreal, Canada, where I was an undergraduate student for 3 years. I love Montreal, and still dearly miss it. The harsh winters take a toll, but they also create a strong complicity between inhabitants, and with the city as a whole.

After some adventures in North America post-graduation, the next big step was New Zealand, where I stayed close to 6 months, on a Working Holiday Visa. I hopped from job to job, hitch-hiking and staying in hostels, and had one of the best times of my life.

After 4 years abroad, I came back to Paris, where I'm now a student. Despite going away, there's no denying that Paris is my home, and that I'm Parisian at heart. At the time of writing this, I am leaving for a year, and will leave again in the future, but Paris will always remain my hometown.
